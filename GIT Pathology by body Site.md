### Oesophagus

* Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease (GORD) and hiatal hernia[^1]&#x20;
* Oesophagitis: reflux, eosinophilic, infectious (Candida/HSV/CMV), chemical/radiation[^1]&#x20;
* Barrett’s oesophagus (intestinal metaplasia) → dysplasia → adenocarcinoma[^1]&#x20;
* Oesophageal squamous cell carcinoma (SCC)[^1]&#x20;
* Oesophageal varices[^1]&#x20;
* Schatzki rings/webs; achalasia; Mallory–Weiss tears; Boerhaave perforation; congenital/functional disorders[^1]&#x20;

### Stomach

* Acute & chronic gastritis: H. pylori, autoimmune, chemical/drug-related (reactive gastropathy included)[^2]&#x20;
* Peptic ulcer disease (gastric & duodenal)[^2]&#x20;
* Gastric polyps: hyperplastic, fundic gland; gastric adenoma[^2]&#x20;
* Gastric adenocarcinoma (intestinal vs diffuse/signet-ring; linitis plastica)[^2]&#x20;
* Gastric MALT lymphoma[^2]&#x20;
* Gastrointestinal stromal tumour (GIST)[^2]&#x20;

### Small intestine (duodenum/jejunum/ileum)

* Small bowel obstruction[^3]&#x20;
* Meckel’s diverticulum (rule of 2s)[^3]&#x20;
* Intestinal ischaemia[^3]&#x20;
* Coeliac disease (gluten-sensitive enteropathy)[^3]&#x20;
* Autoimmune enteropathy; IPEX variant[^3]&#x20;
* Common variable immunodeficiency (CVID) enteropathy[^3]&#x20;
* Giardiasis[^3]&#x20;
* Small-intestinal neoplasia: adenocarcinoma, neuroendocrine tumour, GIST, lymphoma; metastases (e.g., melanoma)[^3]&#x20;

### Large intestine (colon & rectum)

**Non-neoplastic**

* Inflammatory bowel disease: ulcerative colitis; Crohn disease; indeterminate colitis[^4]&#x20;
* Microscopic colitis (lymphocytic, collagenous)[^4]&#x20;
* Infectious colitides; pseudomembranous colitis (C. difficile)[^4]&#x20;
* Ischaemic colitis; angiodysplasia[^4]&#x20;
* Diverticular disease; SCAD (segmental colitis associated with diverticulosis)[^4]&#x20;
* Irritable bowel syndrome (functional)[^4]&#x20;

**Neoplastic**

* Adenomas (tubular/tubulovillous/villous) and adenoma–carcinoma sequence; malignant polyp features[^5] &#x20;
* Colorectal adenocarcinoma pathways: CIN (APC→KRAS→TP53), MSI/MMR deficiency, CIMP/BRAF subset[^5] &#x20;
* Non-epithelial tumours: neuroendocrine tumour/carcinoma, lymphoma, MiNEN[^5]&#x20;

### Appendix

* Acute appendicitis[^6]&#x20;

### Liver

* Hepatitis (viral/autoimmune): interface hepatitis; HBV “ground-glass” cells[^7]&#x20;
* Steatosis & steatohepatitis (alcohol-related features: Mallory’s hyaline, neutrophils)[^7]&#x20;
* Cholestasis patterns (feathery degeneration, bile plugs; bile ductule proliferation)[^7]&#x20;
* Primary biliary cholangitis (PBC) staging; primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC)[^7]&#x20;
* Cirrhosis (final common pathway; nodular fibrosis)[^8]&#x20;

**Liver/biliary neoplasia (from HPB tumours)**

* Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC); hepatocellular adenoma (HCA subtypes)[^9]&#x20;
* Cholangiocarcinoma[^9]&#x20;

### Biliary tree & Gallbladder

* Ascending cholangitis (noted in overlap of site problems)[^6]&#x20;
* PSC/PBC (see above under Liver—cholestatic diseases affecting bile ducts)[^7]&#x20;

### Pancreas

* Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC); PanIN precursor lesions[^9]&#x20;
* Pancreatic cystic neoplasms: MCN, IPMN, IOPN[^9]&#x20;
* Pancreatic neuroendocrine tumours (well-differentiated NETs) vs neuroendocrine carcinomas (NECs)[^9]&#x20;
