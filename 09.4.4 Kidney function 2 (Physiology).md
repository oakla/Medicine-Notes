## Tubular Reabsorption

* **Highly selective.**

  * 100% glucose, nearly all amino acids.
  * \~99% salts and water.
  * Wastes (creatinine, much urea) not reabsorbed.
* **Mechanisms:**

  * **Active transport:** Na+, H+, K+, Ca2+, Mg2+.
  * **Secondary active transport:** glucose, amino acids, Clâˆ’, H+ (coupled with Na+).
  * **Pinocytosis:** small proteins (<50 kDa).
  * **Passive transport:** Clâˆ’, HCO3âˆ’, urea, water.
  * **Osmosis:** water follows Na+ (aquaporins, solvent drag).

## Countercurrent Systems

* **Countercurrent Multiplier (Loop of Henle):**

  * Descending limb: water leaves, fluid equilibrates with interstitium.
  * Thick ascending limb: active Na+K+2Clâˆ’ transport, impermeable to water â†’ dilutes tubular fluid, concentrates interstitium.
* **Urea Recirculation:**

  * With ADH, urea diffuses from inner medullary CD â†’ interstitium â†’ LoH â†’ back to CD.

* **Vasa Recta (Countercurrent Exchanger):**
